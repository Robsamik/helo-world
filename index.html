Harvesting and Preparing Polk Salad for the Table

Polk Salad can be found in Wood lots, along fence lines, and growing against buildings or anywhere else it's able to mature and replenish. The berries turn purple and then fall to the ground in the fall and then the sprouts come up in the spring.

Polk Salad is best in the early spring when the leaves are tender but you can pick it up I'm up till it gets berries but don't eat it after it gets berries because it is poisonous.

I harvest the leaves and the stalks on polk salad usually about the size of the ones in the photo above note: the smaller plant these are more tender so I boil them in a regular pot, and the bigger ones three to four feet tall I pressurize in a pressure cooker.

Wash Your Polk Salad in the Sink

I fill my sink halfway with Polk Salad covered in water swirling around a few times then I drain the water and hand rinse a little at a time while putting in the opressure cooker pot

Boil AND DRAIN Polk Salad 3 Times

I fill a 12-quart pressure cooker 3 quarters full of greens submerged in water and at medium heat bring to a boil for 15 to 20 minutes, drain and then rinse in cold tap water. his shrinks the greens down to where I can put them in a smaller pot about a half a gallon then I bring back to a boil and drain 2 more times

The young tender plants I don't use the pressure cooker just a regular pot
